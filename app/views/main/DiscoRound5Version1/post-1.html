ReadingCat

We do daily toothbrushing sessions in our nursery for the children that are old enough. Each child has a toothbrush and toothpaste labelled with their own name and all of us have done training so we can make sure they’re doing it properly, good technique and all that. It was a good refresher for some of us too! We have a chart on the wall where the children mark when they’ve brushed their teeth.

We’ve also done activities where we get children to use a mirror to look inside their mouths and tell us what they can see, count their teeth etc. There are some good resources online for oral health.

Department for Education

Hi yorkshire_childminder81. Oral health has been included in the EYFS framework because good oral health habits need to be formed from the earliest age. Tooth decay is largely preventable, but is still a serious problem among young children.

Oral health is part of the welfare and safeguarding requirements in the new EYFS rather than being part of the areas of learning. It is up to you to decide how you do this, based on what will work best in your setting. Ideally oral health can be linked to self-care, healthy eating and physical development that you are already promoting with your children.

Some things you can do are:

  • suggest they brush dolls’ teeth, or soft toys’ teeth
  • explore stories about teeth and smiles
  • talk about food that helps the growth of strong teeth, and food that doesn’t
  • talk about teeth brushing as the children arrive
  • try to provide parents and carers with the right knowledge and practical advice to support oral health at home

Visiting dentists, tooth brushing kits or tooth brushing routines will all support the children’s growing awareness of oral health.

You do not have to carry out supervised toothbrushing or assess children’s oral health. However, if you decide to bring in supervised toothbrushing there are various schemes that support this. You may want to speak to your local authority about this.

If supervised toothbrushing is something you decide to introduce, you should also read the guidance on supervised toothbrushing during coronavirus (COVID-19).

You can read more about oral health on the help for early years providers website.
